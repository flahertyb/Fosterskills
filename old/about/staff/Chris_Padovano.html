Chris Padovano
Senior Vice President of Corporate Relations

Born in 1988 and raised in Brentwood NY, Chris has always had a passion for social justice. By way his work through the Long Island Food Bank and the Roxbury YMCA, Chris developed an interest in adoption law. Specifically, he has been researching the Adoption and Safe Families Act(1997) in trying to facilitate better communication between federal and state adoption agencies in order to ensure that the mental and physical security of adopted children is protected. After collaborating with both Marquis and Spencer previously, Chris signed on to Foster Skills intending to make a real difference in the lives of children.

Chris is responsible for creating business processes that further advance Foster Skills mission and, more importantly, building corporate relationships with multinational organizations.

Chris has gained firsthand financial regulation experience working at JP Morgan. He has performed compliance duties for mutual and hedge fund clients. Prior to JP Morgan Chris worked for PC Gibbons, The Law Office Of Jeffery Golkin, and The Fox Hollow Farm. At Gibbons PC, Chris worked as a Business Commercial Litigation Intern on a patent litigation case involving one-piece offender tracking technology. Chris also worked as a Tax-Law Intern assisting clients with tax forms, general tax filings, and real estate matters. Chris knows what it's like to work hard having experienced the rigors of farm life. He worked on the Fox Hollow Farm as a farm foreman with various duties including, amongst other things, digging, planting, tilling, and landscaping. He plans on bringing the same work ethic to help Foster Skills grow.
