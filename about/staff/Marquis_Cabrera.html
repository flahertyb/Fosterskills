Marquis Cabrera
President and Founder


Why Foster Skills?
The odds facing children in foster care are grave. Having been through the foster care system, Marquis believes it is his duty to create opportunities for foster youth. Marquis says, "I founded Foster Skills to create collaborations between all parties and persons working to tackle issues facing children in foster care - and more importantly develop an effective team equipped to help foster youth achieve the American Dream."

Outside of Foster Skills:
Marquis Cabrera is a native of Scotchtown, New York. He studied criminology with a concentration in juvenile law and policy at Northeastern University, earning a Bachelors of Science magna cum laude. Mr. Cabrera worked in government, business, and nonprofit sectors. He built a computer program that created guest and student IDs that scanned to increase school district security for The Enlarged City School District of Middletown. He also worked in the HP (HR) department at City Year, and he was integral to the recruitment business process. He served as an operations and legal policy intern in the White House Office of the Chief of Staff. There, he worked on economic, energy, privacy, and fiscal policy issues. His long-term project was leading White House efforts to help the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) stand up a series of online seminars to train sub- and primary-recipients of Recovery Act funding to comply with the reporting requirements of the M-09-21 guidance. He currently sits on the board for MutinySoft, and USAdelante.

Fun fact:
He has been snowboarding for 10 years; and loves, loves the X-Games.
