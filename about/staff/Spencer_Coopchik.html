Spencer Coopchik
Senior Vice President of Strategy and Development


At an early age, Spencer developed a passion to help children. As a Connecticut native, Spencer has a long history of nonprofit work, starting with the establishment of his own foundation in high school. He has continued his nonprofit involvement while attending Northeastern University. "I am excited about the work of this organization and the work ethic of the staff is contagious. There are definitely movers and shakers in this group, and I am committed to changing the Foster Care System."

Spencer is responsible for maintaining a working knowledge of current events in the happenings of foster care and creating strategic initiatives around making sure Foster Skills develops into a household name. However, more importantly, Spencer is responsible for fundraising strategies and managing the fundraising committee.

Spencer has lobbied on behalf of Massachusetts foster youth and successful assisted in passing Senate Bill 40, in 2010. In addition, he volunteered at the Home For Little Wanderers, and the Boston Rescue Mission. He co-founded the Boston Dialectical Society with Cabrera and Padovano, and he has worked to help mentor students of the Boston Debate League. He has been dedicated to promoting the efforts of Foster Skills.
